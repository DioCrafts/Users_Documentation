# Data Catalog & glossary

The **Data Catalog** is your shared business glossary — a governed set of agreed
terms with clear definitions, so everyone means the same thing by "beneficial owner"
or "structuring". Terms can be tagged to objects and cross-referenced to outside
standards.

## Browsing the glossary

Open **Catalog** and you'll find a search box and a grid of **term cards**. Each card
shows the term, a classification badge, and the start of its definition. Click a card
to expand it.

The expanded view shows:

- the **full definition**,
- the **objects tagged** with this term — entities in your workspace this term
  applies to, each clickable,
- **crosswalks** — semantic links to other terms, including ones in external standard
  vocabularies (for example, "this term means the same as that industry term").

## Searching

- **Lexical** search matches the words you type.
- **Semantic** search (when available) also finds terms that mean something similar,
  even if they don't share the same words — useful when you know the concept but not
  the exact label.

Toggle between the two with the search-mode switch, then search.

::: tip Classification applies here too
You only see terms at or below your clearance. A term marked SECRET simply won't
appear for a CONFIDENTIAL user, the same as any other object.
:::

## Adding terms (administrators)

Administrators can click **New term**, enter the name and definition, choose a
classification, and save. The term then appears in the glossary, ready to be tagged
to objects and cross-referenced.

## Recipes

**Look up what a term officially means.**
Open Catalog → search the term → read its definition → see which objects it's tagged
to so you can jump straight to examples.

**Find the right term for a concept.**
Switch to **Semantic** search → type the idea in your own words (for example,
"hiding the true owner") → review the suggested terms → open the best match.

**Define a new standard term (admin).**
**New term** → name it "Beneficial Owner" → write a clear definition → set the
classification → save. Everyone now shares one definition.
