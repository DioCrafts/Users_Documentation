# Finding your way around

AXIOM has a lot of modules, but the frame around them is simple and consistent.
Learn the sidebar, the top bar, and the search box, and you can reach anything in a
couple of clicks.

## The sidebar (the rail)

The narrow strip down the left edge is your **navigation rail**. Each icon is a
module. The AXIOM mark at the very top always takes you **Home**.

- **Hover** an icon to see its name and a one-line description.
- **Click** to open the module.
- **Collapse / Expand** (bottom of the rail) widens the rail to show full names and
  descriptions alongside the icons. Your choice is remembered.

### Pin only what you use

![The "All modules" sheet with pin toggles](/images/nav-rail-more.png)
*The "All modules" sheet: browse every module grouped by purpose and pin the ones you use.*

AXIOM has more modules than anyone needs every day, so the rail shows only your
**pinned** modules. Everything else is one click away:

1. Click **More** at the bottom of the rail to open the **All modules** sheet.
2. Browse modules grouped by purpose (Workspace, Explore & Model, Analyze, Decide &
   Act, Integrate, Govern).
3. Click the **pin** icon on any module to keep it in your rail, or unpin one you
   don't use.
4. Click a module's name to jump straight to it.

Nothing is ever hidden — unpinned modules are always reachable through **More** and
through search. Pinning just keeps your everyday set within reach. Your pins are
remembered in your browser.

The module you're currently in always appears in the rail and is highlighted, so
"where am I?" is always answered.

## The top bar

Across the top of every screen:

- **Project picker (far left).** Shows your current focus — a project, or
  "All objects". Click it to switch projects, create a new one, or jump to the full
  Projects list. See [Projects](/guide/projects).
- **Breadcrumb.** Shows the path to where you are (group › module › detail), so you
  always know your place.
- **Search box (centre).** Click it or press <kbd>⌘K</kbd> (Mac) / <kbd>Ctrl-K</kbd>
  (Windows/Linux) to open the command palette.
- **Copilot button.** Opens the AI assistant. See [AI Copilot & agents](/guide/ai).
- **Language toggle (EN / ES).** Switches the whole interface between English and
  Spanish.
- **Theme toggle (moon / sun).** Switches between dark and light.
- **Notifications bell.** A dot appears when you have unread notifications.
- **Account menu (your initials).** Change your password or sign out.

## Search and the command palette

The search box is the fastest way to get anywhere. Press <kbd>⌘K</kbd> from any
screen and a panel opens that does three jobs at once:

![The command palette opened with a search term](/images/command-palette.png)
*The command palette (⌘K): find objects, run quick actions, and jump to any screen.*

1. **Find objects.** Start typing a name (or a detail like a phone number or IBAN)
   and matching objects appear instantly, each with its type and risk. Click one to
   open its 360° profile.
2. **Run an action.** Quick actions like **New project**, **Open Reports**,
   **Ask the Copilot**, **Connect a data source**, **Toggle theme**, and
   **Show keyboard shortcuts** are listed — pick one to do it immediately.
3. **Go to a screen.** Every module is listed under **Go to**. Type part of a name
   (in English or Spanish) to filter. Sub-screens appear as "Parent › Lens" so you
   can land exactly where you mean to.

Press <kbd>Enter</kbd> to see **all results** for what you typed on a full search
page. Press <kbd>Esc</kbd> to close the palette.

::: tip Search respects your access
Search only ever returns objects you're cleared to see. If the data can't be
reached, the palette shows an honest "Offline" or error badge instead of guessing.
:::

## Notifications

Click the **bell** to open your notifications drawer. You'll find:

- **Alerts** that have fired,
- **Actions** waiting for your approval, or actions you proposed that were decided,
- **Mentions** of you in reports.

Each notification links straight to the thing it's about — click it to jump there
(and it's marked read). Use **Mark all read** to clear the unread count. The drawer
shows how long ago each item arrived.

## The active project, everywhere

Your chosen project quietly shapes the whole app: lists, search, new objects, and
new work products all default to it. This is why the project picker sits front and
centre. When you want the widest possible view, switch the picker to **All objects**.
See [Projects](/guide/projects) for the details.

## Keyboard shortcuts

A few shortcuts speed everything up:

| Keys | Does |
|---|---|
| <kbd>⌘K</kbd> / <kbd>Ctrl-K</kbd> | Open search & command palette |
| <kbd>⌘J</kbd> / <kbd>Ctrl-J</kbd> | Open the Copilot |
| <kbd>?</kbd> | Show all keyboard shortcuts |
| <kbd>Esc</kbd> | Close the current overlay |

The graph and other canvases have their own navigation keys. The full list is in
[Keyboard shortcuts](/guide/keyboard-shortcuts).

## Getting help when a screen looks empty

If a list is empty, AXIOM tells you why rather than leaving you guessing:

- **"All caught up" / "No … yet"** — there's genuinely nothing here; you may need to
  create or import something.
- **An "Offline" badge** — AXIOM can't reach your data right now; try again shortly.
- **A locked / "administrator access required" message** — the screen needs a role or
  clearance you don't have. Ask your administrator if you think you should have it.
