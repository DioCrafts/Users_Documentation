# Watchlists

A **watchlist** keeps the objects you care about within easy reach. Whether you're
monitoring a handful of high-risk entities or scanning a whole category, watchlists
turn a sprawling data set into a focused, glanceable list.

## Two kinds of "watching"

- **Your personal watchlist.** From any object's [360° profile](/guide/objects) — or
  from a list — click the **bookmark / Watch** toggle to add it. It's your private
  shortlist; toggle it off to drop the object. Watched objects are highlighted (for
  example, with a marker on the [graph](/guide/graph)) so they stand out.
- **Curated watch views.** The Watchlists module ships with ready-made views that
  group objects by category so you can monitor a whole class at once.

## The Watchlists screen

![The Watchlists module with preset views](/images/watchlists.png)
*Watchlists: switch between ready-made views to monitor whole categories at a glance.*

Open **Watchlists** and you'll see a set of preset views to choose between, plus a
filterable list of the objects in the selected view. Typical presets include:

| View | Shows |
|---|---|
| **On watch** | Everything currently on watch |
| **Maritime watch** | Vessels, ports, and operators under watch |
| **Financial watch** | Accounts, organizations, and transactions under watch |
| **Sanctions** | Entities matched against sanctions lists |
| **High risk 80+** | Every object with a risk score of 80 or more |
| **OFAC / EU matches** | Persons and companies matching OFAC or EU lists |

Pick a preset to filter the list. Within it you can **search** by name or detail and
**sort** (for example, by risk). Click any object to open its full profile.

## Recipes

**Monitor your high-risk portfolio.**
Open **High risk 80+** → scan from the top (highest risk first) → click any entity to
review its connections and flags → add the ones that warrant follow-up to a case or
escalate them.

**Keep an eye on a single entity.**
Open an object's profile → click **Watch** → it joins your personal watchlist. Return
any time via the **On watch** view.

**Check sanctions exposure.**
Open the **Sanctions** or **OFAC / EU matches** view → review each match → open the
profile to see *why* it matched and decide whether to confirm or dismiss it.

::: tip Watchlists respect your access
A watchlist only ever lists objects you're cleared to see. Counts and contents are
tailored to you, just like everywhere else in AXIOM.
:::
