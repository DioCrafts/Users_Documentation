# Reports & Notepad

Two tools capture your thinking and turn it into something you can share. The
**Notepad** is for quick, personal capture. **Reports** are collaborative, polished
documents — with live objects, charts, comments, version history, and PDF export —
that you share with your team.

## Notepad

Open **Notepad** for a personal, free-form notebook. It's not tied to a project, and
it saves itself as you type.

- A list of your notes sits on the left; **New note** starts a blank one.
- The editor on the right is rich text: headings, lists, to-dos, quotes, callouts,
  tables, images, and inline references to objects. Press <kbd>/</kbd> to open a menu
  and insert any block without leaving the keyboard.
- Mention an object (for example, by typing its name) to embed a **live link** to it,
  clickable straight to its [360° profile](/guide/objects).
- A **classification** selector keeps the note at the right sensitivity (capped to
  your clearance), and a status badge shows it's **autosaved** (or warns if you're
  offline — your text isn't lost).
- When a note is ready to become official, **Promote to report** copies it into a
  project as a full report.

### Recipe: capture as you investigate

**New note** → title it → jot findings, using <kbd>/</kbd> for structure → mention the
key entities so they auto-link → later, **Promote to report** to turn it into a shared
deliverable.

## Reports

![The Reports module](/images/reports.png)
*Reports: collaborative, versioned documents with live objects, charts, and PDF export.*

Open **Reports** for collaborative documents that live inside a project. Pick a
project, then create a report from a blank page or a template.

### Building a report from blocks

A report is made of **blocks** you stack and edit in place:

| Block | Use it for |
|---|---|
| **Heading** | Section titles (they form the contents on export) |
| **Text / Rich text** | Narrative, with bold/italics, links, math, images, and inline object references |
| **Callout** | A highlighted note (info, warning, danger) |
| **Live objects** | An embedded, live grid of objects from the project |
| **Chart** | A live chart built like in [ad-hoc analysis](/guide/analytics) — pick a dimension and measure |

Insert blocks with the **+** controls; edit any block by clicking it. The report
**autosaves** as you work, with a status badge.

### Collaborating

- **Comments** — open the comments panel to discuss the report or a specific block,
  and **@mention** teammates (only those cleared for the report are offered). You can
  see who else is viewing it right now.
- **Versions** — every save is snapshotted. Open the versions panel to compare any two
  versions (what was added, removed, changed, or moved) and **Restore** an earlier one
  — the restore is itself saved, so nothing is ever lost.
- **Mentions** reach people through their notifications, taking them straight to the
  spot they were mentioned.

### Classification and redaction

A report carries a **classification** (capped to your clearance). When you export or
share it, anyone who isn't cleared to see an embedded object sees it as **redacted**
rather than revealed — the document adapts to each reader's access automatically.

### Exporting

Click **Export PDF** for a printable dossier: all blocks rendered, charts run with
live data, and objects redacted where the reader isn't cleared. This is your shareable
deliverable, with the audit trail intact behind it.

### Templates

Administrators can provide governed report **templates** so common dossiers start with
the right structure and starter text. You'll only see templates at or below your
clearance.

### Recipe: write and share a dossier

Open Reports → pick the project → **New** (blank or template) → add a **Heading**, a
**Rich text** intro, a **Live objects** block with the key entities, and a **Chart**
→ open **Comments** and **@mention** your reviewer → check **Versions** as drafts
evolve → **Export PDF** to hand off.
